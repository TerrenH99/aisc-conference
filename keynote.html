About Our Keynote Speakers

The 2026 AISC Conference is honored to feature world-renowned keynote speakers who are leaders in their respective fields. These distinguished researchers will present cutting-edge work that shapes the future of statistics, data science, and combinatorics.

Each keynote address offers a unique opportunity to learn from experts who have made significant contributions to advancing statistical methodology and its applications across diverse domains.

John Stufken

Professor, George Mason University

John Stufken is an expert in experimental design and data science, known for his work on orthogonal arrays, crossover designs, and optimal design methods. He has authored influential books and held leadership roles at major universities and the NSF. Stufken is an Elected Fellow of the American Statistical Association and the Institute of Mathematical Statistics.

Dr. Karen Kafadar

Commonwealth Professor, University of Virginia

Professor Kafadar is a leading expert in exploratory data analysis and robust methods. Her research focuses on uncertainty characterization in physical and biological sciences, earning awards from the CDC, ASA, and American Society for Quality.
