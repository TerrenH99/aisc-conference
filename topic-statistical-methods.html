Statistical Methods

Advancing the frontiers of experimental design, causal inference, and survey methodology

Overview

The Statistical Methods track at the 2026 AISC Conference brings together leading researchers and practitioners to explore cutting-edge developments in foundational statistical methodologies. This track covers three essential pillars of modern statistics: Causal Inference, Design of Experiments, and Survey Sampling.

These fundamental methods continue to evolve in response to new challenges in data collection, analysis, and interpretation. Our sessions will showcase innovative approaches that address contemporary issues while maintaining rigorous theoretical foundations.

Key Topics

Causal Inference

Explore advanced methods for establishing causal relationships from observational and experimental data, including propensity score methods, instrumental variables, difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity designs, and synthetic control methods.

Design of Experiments

Discover optimal experimental designs for modern applications, including factorial designs, response surface methodology, robust parameter design, sequential experimentation, and computer experiments.

Survey Sampling

Learn about contemporary approaches to survey design and analysis, including complex sampling designs, small area estimation, nonresponse adjustment, data integration methods, and adaptive survey designs.

Real-World Applications

Statistical methods have profound impacts across numerous fields. Sessions in this track will feature applications in:

  • Public Health: Clinical trials, epidemiological studies, and health policy evaluation
  • Social Sciences: Program evaluation, policy analysis, and social experiments
  • Industry: Quality improvement, product development, and process optimization
  • Agriculture: Field trials, variety testing, and precision agriculture
  • Government: Census methodology, official statistics, and survey research
  • Environmental Science: Environmental monitoring and impact assessment

Why Attend These Sessions?

Whether you're a researcher developing new methodologies, a practitioner applying statistical methods to solve real problems, or a student learning the fundamentals, the Statistical Methods track offers valuable insights into both theoretical advances and practical applications. Engage with experts, discover new techniques, and network with professionals who share your interests in rigorous statistical methodology.

Session Formats

The Statistical Methods track will feature diverse presentation formats to maximize learning and engagement:

  • Invited Sessions: Leading experts present cutting-edge research and methodological advances
  • Contributed Sessions: Researchers share recent findings and innovative applications
  • Poster Presentations: Interactive discussions of new methods and case studies
  • Panel Discussions: Debates on current challenges and future directions in the field
